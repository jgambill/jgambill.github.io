I obtained my Master of Science degree in Information Science from the School of Information Sciences (SIS) at the University of Tennessee-Knoxville (UTK) in 2007. I completed my degree via their distance education program, only stepping onto campus for orientation, for my comprehensive exam, and for graduation (optional). It was a great program in that I didn’t have to uproot my family or leave my library job to complete my degree. I truly do not feel like I missed out on anything by not being on campus – I learned a lot and made both professional contacts and great friends.

Course List

  • Information Environment (IS 510)
  • Information Access and Retrieval (IS 520)
  • Cataloging and Classification (IS 521)
  • Information Representation and Organization (IS 530)
  • Advanced Information Retrieval (IS 535)
  • Research Methods for Information Professionals (IS 540)
  • Management of Information Organizations (IS 550)
  • Academic Libraries (IS 552)
  • Development and Management of Collections (IS 560)
  • Digital Libraries (IS 565)
  • Information Technologies (IS 585)
  • Mining the Web (IS 587)
  • Project Management (IS 590 Special Topics)
  • Practicum (IS 599)

After graduation, I served on the UTK SIS Alumni Board in 2009 – 2010, at which time the board was disbanded.
